Inference as Currency

A monetary theory of the AI cycle, in three pages.

May 2026 1,567 words · 7 min 9 citations

Microsoft cancelled its internal Claude Code seats this May. Microsoft owns Azure, holds 19% of OpenAI, and sells more enterprise software than anyone — the most-aligned buyer for token-priced AI that exists. If the math doesn’t clear there, the buyer-side problem is structural. Same month: Uber burned its entire 2026 AI budget in four months; GitHub deprecated flat-rate Copilot; Anthropic moved from a token meter to opaque weekly caps; OpenAI offered $2M in tokens for equity to every founder at a YC dinner.

The consensus view writes much larger numbers. Trillion-dollar lab revenues by 2030, with “AI finance ministers and central bankers” driving 10% GDP growth in the developing world;1 labs priced on Netflix-and-Spotify comparables, durable margins, personalisation moat, the commoditisation thesis “more or less irrelevant”;2 an $800B market the lab’s seed investors call “a gross underestimate.”3 None of those projections survives this month’s data.

AI labs are private currency issuers. The currency is the token. The underlying asset is the verifier substrate — the human or software that decides whether AI work was done correctly. Token value behaves like nineteenth-century private banknote value: face inside the issuer’s perimeter, discounted with distance, vulnerable to runs when redemption fails. The rent on a token tracks the K-complexity of verification in the buyer’s domain, not model capability and not token volume.

The token

A token is a claim on inference, redeemable through a substrate: a compiler accepts the code, a partner signs the brief, a clinician signs the chart, a regulator approves the filing.

Kolmogorov complexity of verification, \(K(\text{verify})\), is the length of the shortest program that maps an output to a yes-or-no judgment given the verifier’s information set \(I_v\):4

\[K(\text{verify} \mid t, I_v) \;=\; \min_p\, |p| \quad \text{s.t.} \quad U(p,\, y,\, t,\, I_v) = \mathbb{1}\!\left[y \models t\right].\]

For a compiler, \(I_v\) is language semantics plus a test harness — \(K(\text{verify})\) is small. For a partner reviewing a brief, \(I_v\) is the assigned judge’s record, opposing counsel’s playbook, the client’s risk tolerance, the firm’s strategy — \(K(\text{verify})\) is enormous and mostly tacit. The rent on a token tracks \(K(\text{verify})\), not capability:

\(K(\text{verify})\) Verifier Rent accrues to
Small Compiler, test harness, schema Compute owner
Medium Domain software, structured audit Substrate owner
Large Credentialed human, signed + indemnified The signature

Discount by distance

A token from Anthropic redeems at face inside Claude Code. The same token used in a third-party app without Anthropic’s vertical integration redeems at a discount; in a generic domain without a productisable verifier it doesn’t redeem at all.

The pattern is nineteenth-century banknote economics.5 A note from an Indiana bank traded at face in its home county, at a discount in the next state, and as wallpaper across the Mississippi. Free Banking America (1837–1863) produced 800+ private currency issuers; the National Banking Acts of 1863–1864 unified the system. Formally:

\[V(d) \;=\; V_0 \cdot e^{-\lambda d}.\]

\(d\) is institutional distance from the lab’s perimeter and \(\lambda\) is the lab-specific discount rate. The lab’s strategic problem is

\[\min_{\lambda(\cdot),\, D}\; \int_0^{D} \lambda(d)\, \mathrm{d}d \quad \text{s.t.} \quad C(\lambda, D) \leq B,\]

a perimeter-investment budget \(B\) on cost surface \(C(\cdot)\).6 Two corner solutions: deepen (low \(\lambda\), narrow \(D\)) or extend (moderate \(\lambda\), wide \(D\)).

Two strategies

Anthropic deepens. Direct enterprise contracts in regulated verticals — clinical decision support, claims adjudication, regulatory affairs — account for roughly 80% of revenue. First-party harnesses (Claude Code, Coefficient) capture token volume inside the lab. OAuth restrictions pull telemetry inside the perimeter. The bet: \(K(\text{verify})\) stays high; residual rent accrues to substrate owners.

OpenAI extends.7 Multi-vendor supply with Microsoft, Amazon, Google flattens hyperscaler dependence. A horizontal API business prioritises developer adoption. The $2M YC token offer buys equity in tomorrow’s app businesses paid in OpenAI’s own currency. The bet: \(K(\text{verify})\) falls; volume across many small substrates dominates depth in any one.

Anthropic — deepen OpenAI — extend
Perimeter narrow, high-investment wide, low-investment
Revenue mix ~80% enterprise verticals API + capacity reservations
K(verify) bet stays high falls
Token sink first-party harnesses third-party apps + YC equity
Failure mode vertical gets disrupted volume base commoditises

Both are autarky. The labs are not competing for the same customer. They are betting on opposite futures of the same parameter.

Bank runs

Microsoft’s cancellation is a single-buyer redemption failure — a depositor turned away. The bank survives the withdrawal. Information about whether it could honour at face propagates. Other large enterprises run the same cost-benefit math. Enough simultaneous cancellations and the lab faces a coordinated run. Let \(R_b\) be the cost to construct buyer \(b\)’s redemption infrastructure and \(V_b\) the realised per-token value; \(b\) cancels when

\[R_b \;>\; V_b \;=\; V_0 \cdot e^{-\lambda d_b}.\]

The threshold is endogenous in \(\lambda\). Weekly rate caps and priority-service contracts push pricing into bilateral capacity reservations — the retreat Free Banking-era banks made when they ran out of specie.8

Episode Backing Outcome
TerraUSD (May 2022) algorithmic — none $45B destroyed, 1 week
USDC (Mar 2023) full but SVB-exposed depeg, 2 days, restored
Tether (2016–18) 27.6% (NY AG settlement) survived; never fully audited

Private currency without sufficient backing fails not gradually but suddenly, when buyer confidence cracks.

Resolution

Path Mechanism Analog Trigger
State imposition Regulated-issuer framework; USG consortium plausible9 National Banking Acts 1863–64 Visible run + geopolitics
Serial failure Smaller labs fail or merge; survivors compress to defense-contractor multiples UK 19th-century banking Time-gap stress
Multipolar fragmentation US, Chinese, EU, Indian, Gulf systems; cross-border verifier-credentialing the binding institution Pre-1914 gold-standard era Status quo extends

Probability is roughly equal across the three on a five-year horizon. The realised outcome will be a combination.

What this means

Lab equity is priced on platform-software comparables. Realised revenue and margin look like cost-pass-through infrastructure. Multiple compression from platform to monetary-institution multiples is the modal outcome, not a tail risk.

The institutions that benefit are verifier-substrate owners — credentialing bodies, audit firms, vertical software that owns the substrate in its domain (Halter, Waymo, OpenEvidence, Sardine, Abridge). Hardware and energy infrastructure benefits regardless of resolution path.

The credentialed human at the bottom of the signature chain is the closest thing the cycle has produced to specie reserves. She holds value across every redemption scenario; no lab can capture her without integrating into the substrate where her signature lives. The wage of the doctor, the lawyer, the engineer, the underwriter, the pilot rises in real terms over five years. The middle of the labour market — the analyst, the coordinator, the entry-level associate — compresses out of every column simultaneously. The political debate about AI and labour has been sighting the wrong tier.

The cycle has been pricing the wrong substrate.
